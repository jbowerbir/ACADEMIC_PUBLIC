# Earning Our Place, More or Less: How Americans Define What the Rich and Poor Deserve

Jacob S. Bower-Bir (2020). *Economia Politica*, 37(3), 861-878.

## Abstract

What do Americans think determines whether someone deserves to be rich or poor? This paper presents survey evidence on 15 factors Americans rate as important in determining wealth and poverty. The central finding: personal responsibility has a flexible, not fixed, relationship with desert. Americans accept unearned advantages (intelligence, creativity, family connections) as desert-worthy even when they are not personally controlled. Elite school graduates define desert differently from the general public — they rate effort-based factors lower and structural factors higher.

## Key Finding

The effort-luck dichotomy (Alesina & Angeletos 2005) that dominates the literature on redistribution preferences is wholly inadequate. Desert is richer than personal responsibility. Americans construct desert definitions that include uncontrolled factors, and these definitions vary systematically by education and class position.
